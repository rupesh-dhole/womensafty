Women's Safety & Legal Awareness

An overview of important resources, rights, and safety measures.

Overview

Ensuring the safety and dignity of women is a national priority. This dashboard provides a consolidated view of critical information, from emergency helplines and practical safety tips to an overview of your legal rights. Empowering women with knowledge is the first step towards creating a safer and more just society. Government and non-governmental organizations are committed to providing support through various initiatives, legal frameworks, and awareness campaigns.

Safety Tips

Physical & Travel Safety

  • Be Aware: Always be conscious of your surroundings and avoid distractions like being on your phone in isolated areas.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or person feels wrong, leave immediately.
  • Share Your Plans: Let a trusted friend or family member know your travel plans, cab details, and expected return time.
  • Avoid Dark Alleys: Stick to well-lit, populated routes, even if it's a longer path.
  • Learn Basic Self-Defense: Knowing a few simple moves can build confidence and be crucial in an emergency.

Online & Digital Safety

  • Strong Passwords: Use unique, complex passwords for each account and enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Private Profiles: Set your social media profiles to private and be selective about who you add as a friend.
  • Beware Phishing: Don't click on suspicious links or download attachments from unknown senders.
  • Limit Personal Info: Avoid sharing sensitive personal information like your full address or phone number publicly.
  • Block & Report: Do not hesitate to block and report any user who sends harassing or inappropriate messages.

Know Your Legal Rights

Right to Equal Pay

The Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, mandates equal pay for men and women for the same work or work of a similar nature.

Against Domestic Violence

The Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, protects against physical, mental, verbal, or economic abuse by a partner or family.

Against Workplace Harassment

The PoSH Act, 2013, provides protection against sexual harassment at the workplace and mandates the formation of an Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).

Right to Free Legal Aid

Under the Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, women (regardless of income) are entitled to free legal services from the state.

Maternity Benefits

The Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 (Amended), provides for 26 weeks of paid maternity leave for women in the organized sector.

Right Against Dowry

The Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, makes the act of giving or taking dowry a punishable offense.
